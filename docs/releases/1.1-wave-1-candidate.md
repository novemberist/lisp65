# lisp65 1.1 Wave 1 candidate notes

Status: **candidate documentation; not a released version**

Wave 1 provides the Attic library shelf, export-only interning, first-class
buffers, compiler-tier deresidentization, native `filter`, and the new REPL
banner. Its product identity and hardware acceptance remain subject to the
R4/R5/R6/G5/G6 promotion chain.

## Known limitation: first call after a definition

On the reference MEGA65, the first expression compiled after a persistent
definition typically takes **1.90 to 1.96 seconds**; occasionally longer times
have been observed. Immediately following warm expressions take about
**0.20 seconds**. This is a dated, owner-approved limitation, not a passed
performance target.

When entering several related definitions, enter them as one block and test
afterward. This amortizes the compiler-tier reload across the block.

The C2 direct-Attic-execution architecture is the committed cure for 1.2. If C2
leaves the 1.2 scope, this exception expires and requires a new explicit owner
decision; it does not renew automatically.

## Buffer display

The REPL prints a Buffer as `?`. Inspect its contents with `buffer-ref` and its
length with `buffer-length`.

## Corrected surface claim

`filter` is delivered in this candidate and covered by the surface-delivery
parity gate. The 1.0.1 `filter` erratum may be retired only after Wave 1 has a
completed hardware seal.
